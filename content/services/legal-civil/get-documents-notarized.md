# Notarial Services — Bacolod City

**Notarization** is the process of having documents authenticated by a **Notary Public** to make them legally binding.

---

## 1. Common Documents That Need Notarization

| Document Type      | Examples                                                                       |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Personal documents | Affidavits, sworn statements, authorization letters, special power of attorney |
| Business documents | Contracts, deeds of sale, lease agreements, partnership agreements             |
| Legal documents    | Affidavit of support, affidavit of consent, deed of donation, waiver           |

---

## 2. Where to Get Documents Notarized

| Location            | Details                                                                | Typical Fee |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| Private law offices | Most lawyers in Bacolod are Notaries Public (near City Hall, downtown) | ₱100-₱500   |
| IBP Bacolod Chapter | May offer notarial services                                            | Varies      |
| Barangay Hall       | Limited to simple affidavits (free or minimal fee)                     | Free-₱50    |

---

## 3. Requirements

**What to Bring:**

- Original document to be notarized (unsigned or already signed)
- Valid government-issued ID with photo and signature
- Community Tax Certificate (Cedula) - required for most notarial acts
- All parties named in the document must appear personally

**Important:** Do NOT sign the document before appearing - you must sign in front of the notary.

---

## 4. Notarization Process

1. Find a Notary Public (law office, IBP, or barangay hall)
2. Bring requirements (document, ID, cedula)
3. Notary reviews document for completeness
4. Sign document in front of the notary
5. Take oath (raise right hand and swear document is true)
6. Notary signs, seals, and dates the document
7. Pay notarial fee
8. Receive notarized document

**Processing Time:** Same day (10-30 minutes)

---

## 5. Typical Notarial Fees in Bacolod

| Document Type                | Typical Fee |
| ---------------------------- | ----------- |
| Simple affidavit (1-2 pages) | ₱100-₱200   |
| Affidavit (3+ pages)         | ₱200-₱500   |
| Contracts and agreements     | ₱200-₱500   |
| Deed of sale (real estate)   | ₱500-₱2,000 |
| Special power of attorney    | ₱200-₱500   |

**Note:** Fees vary by notary and document complexity. Always ask for the fee before proceeding.

## 7. Important Reminders

- Read the document carefully before signing
- Bring valid ID (expired IDs not accepted)
- Appear in person (no representatives allowed)
- Tell the truth - false statements in notarized documents are punishable by law
- Keep original document safely after notarization
